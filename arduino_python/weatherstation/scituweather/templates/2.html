Blast Disease

Symptoms

Symptoms: Initial symptoms are white to gray-green lesions or spots with darker borders produced on all parts of the shoot, while older lesions are elliptical or spindle-shaped and whitish to gray with necrotic borders. Lesions may enlarge and coalesce to kill the entire leaf. It also affects reproduction by causing the host to produce fewer seeds.

Causal agent : Magnaporthe grisea, Pyricularia oryzae (anamorph)
Percentage of damage: Seedling 90%, Grains production period 50%

Managements

1. Using resistance cultivars and seed collection by yourself.
2. Integrated management program should be implemented.
3. Managing the amount of water supplied to the crops limits spore mobility.
4. Biological control such as Trichoderma spp., Bacillus subtilis, Pseudomonas fluorescens.
5. Chemical controls such as carbendazim, kazukamycin, prochloraz, tricyclazol have been shown to prevent penetration of the pathogen.
