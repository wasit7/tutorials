Bacterial Leaf Blight Disease

Symptoms

Lesions are pale-green to grey-green, water-soaked streaks near the leaf tip and margins. In older plants, the leaves become yellow and then die.

Causal agent : Xanthomonas oryzae pv. oryzae
Percentage of damage: Seedling 80%, Grains production period 40%

Managements

1. Planting resistant rice cultivars.
2. Suitable using  nitrogenous fertilizer because the use of excess nitrogenous fertilizer has shown an increase in incidence.
3. Biological control such as Trichoderma spp., Bacillus subtilis, Pseudomonas fluorescens.
4. Chemical controls such as Carbendazim, kazukamycin, prochloraz, tricyclazol have been shown to prevent penetration of the pathogen.
